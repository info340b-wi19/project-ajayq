Project Description

Problem Description

The problem that I want to tackle is the problem of parking in Seattle. The biggest problem with parking in Seattle is being able to reliably find cheap parking on the streets of Seattle. Currently street parking is incredibly inconvenient to find, pretty much the only way to find it are by happening upon a free spot as one drives around. This is not only hard on the person that is doing the searching, but it can actually become hazardous to them and the people around them. Searching for spots can be distracting for the driver, and it can also make them drive slower which can contribute to traffic within the area. The only other option in these scenarios are submitting to an overpriced parking lot, if there even is one that is open. Currently there is no way to find out better information about where one could find street parking. However, there is an application endorsed by the Seattle government called PayByPhone which allows people to:

  • Remotely extend parking session by paying through their phone app.
  • Get textmessage reminders of when their time is about to end.
  • Kepp track of receipts for travel, parking locations, and other things.
There are also other separate applications that one could find online such as ParkMe. These kinds of websites allow people to reserve parking spots at different locations around Seattle's downtown area. However the issue with this website is that the parking spots are not only sparse but they are also more expensive (some more than others) than street parking in the same area.

App Description

The app that I will create will be one that takes the information that the government has regarding whether or not meters still have minutes on them to make an informed guess of whether or not a certain spot is in use or not. I would cater my app towards anyone who uses street parking within Seattle on a regular basis. One group of individuals who this might be especially helpful for are carriers, whether that be people who work for postmates or package/ message carriers. However this would also be helpful to anyone who is not as familiar with Seattle's downtown area as they might be very anxious in this new environment and this would relieve that kind of pressure. Though this might not eliminate the search for spots on the street it could drastically improve people's experience with finding parking in downtown.

App Functionality

  1. The application would open up and ask the user for their desired destination within Seattle.
  2. Then the application would jump to a map of the location and surrounding areas. This would display many dots within the map that represent all of the parking meters. Dots that are lit up would represent the meters that currently do not have any money in them indicating that they are most likely vacant. Certain larger sections of the map may be lit up indicating that there are many meters within that section that are free.
  3. After the user has scrolled around the map and found ether a specific meter or a section that they wished to set as their destination they can click on their desired location. This would then send open up google maps sending them to that specific location.
